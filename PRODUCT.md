# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Marketers, SEO specialists, content strategists, agencies, and in-house growth teams responsible for organic search, paid search, content, social, and competitive intelligence. Two paths converge on the same surface — individual practitioners signing up self-serve, and enterprise buyers booking a sales conversation. Secondary surfaces serve prospective hires, partners, and the press.

The dominant moment is evaluation. Visitors are sizing Semrush against the workflow they already run — keyword research, competitor analysis, site audits, rank tracking, content gap analysis — and against the alternatives they already use. The hero has one job: prove the product is worth the next click.

## Product Purpose

Semrush is the visibility platform for modern search. One surface measures and grows brand presence across AI search, organic, paid, social, and adjacent channels — and connects fifty-plus tools to a single conversion path: enter a URL, see the visibility picture, start a trial.

Five product pillars carry the toolset — Semrush One, the AI Toolkit, .Trends, Enterprise SEO Solutions, and Local SEO. Success is a marketer who lands on the home page, types a domain, gets a credible snapshot of where they stand on organic, paid, and AI-mention share, and converts to a trial that grows with their work.

The voice is direct, current, and unembarrassed by data. Trust comes from third-party validation — G2, Forrester, Forbes Advisor — and from a long roster of customer logos people already recognize.

## Brand Personality

**Technical-confident, bold and direct.**

Semrush is a tool the operator already trusts. The brand assumes domain literacy — keyword strategy, AI Overviews, backlink analytics — without slipping into jargon, and it backs every claim with a tool name, a number, or a customer logo. That technical confidence is the substance.

The register adopts Adobe's bold-direct cadence: single-clause headlines, sentence case, periods inside titles, em-dashes preferred over commas, no exclamation marks. Confident generosity over capability density. The page does not list what it can do — it states what it does, then proves it once.

In three words: **trusted, current, unflinching.**

What the redesigned brand is **not**: not creative-flowery (that would lose semrush's data substance), not a feature catalog (that would keep semrush's current register), and not Adobe-as-Adobe (Acrobat is the design vocabulary, not the brand promise). The honest blend is *technical confidence expressed bold and direct.*

## Anti-references

The redesigned semrush is **not**:

- **A listicle home page.** No "10 reasons why," no "Top 5 SEO mistakes." Headlines are single, declarative, period-terminated. (Anti-pattern from current semrush register; see Acrobat tone evidence.)
- **A capability-dense subhead farm.** Subheads like "The leading platform to grow and measure brand visibility across virtually every digital channel" cataloging features in one breath are out. Sub-decks earn one job: extend the headline by one sentence. (Anti-pattern from current semrush register.)
- **A three-CTA hero.** "Start free trial / Get insights / Talk to sales" stacked in the hero is a B2B funnel reflex. The hero carries one primary action, paired with one secondary at most.
- **Generic SaaS hero with screenshot-of-app dropshadow.** The surface is the product moment, not a chrome simulation. (Preserved verbatim from stardust/current/PRODUCT.md.)
- **Fluffy AI marketing language.** No "revolutionary AI," no "next-gen platform." AI claims anchor to a tool, an integration (ChatGPT, AI Overviews, Perplexity), or a use case. (Preserved verbatim from stardust/current/PRODUCT.md.)
- **Pure-black backgrounds + neon gradients (the "AI startup" cliché).** The page is mostly white. (Preserved.)
- **Badge soup, gradient hero backgrounds, social-proof logo strips immediately under the hero.** The hero is one frame: copy, image surface, two CTAs. Logos earn their own section. (Adopted from DESIGN.md anti-references.)
- **8px-radius rounded rectangles.** All CTAs are pill-shaped. A 6px or 8px radius button signals the wrong system. (Adopted from DESIGN.md.)
- **Adobe.com.** This is a redesign of semrush, not a repaint. If a section reads as if it could describe Adobe — creative tools, design files, PDFs — it is wrong. The substance is still SEO, content, competitive intelligence.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn the viewport.** The page is mostly white. Type is enormous. Color is rationed: near-black ink for buttons, two warm grays for surfaces, one signature blue reserved for the trial CTA. Loud where it counts; quiet everywhere else. *(Adopted from DESIGN.md § Overview — applied as a brand principle, not just a visual one.)*

2. **Single-clause headline. Period.** Section opens are eyebrow + Title 2 + one supporting sentence. No multi-clause runs, no semicolons, no colons-as-headlines. The headline is the statement; the eyebrow is the category cue; the deck extends by one sentence at most. *(Adopted from DESIGN.md § Eyebrow-Headline Pair Rule.)*

3. **Tool name first, capability second — but don't list.** Feature surfaces lead with the named product (Keyword Magic Tool, Site Audit, Traffic Analytics) and one verb-led sentence about what it does. Names earn their own line; capability cards are not bullet shelves. *(Substance preserved from stardust/current/PRODUCT.md design principle 3; register sharpened — no more capability-dense subheads.)*

4. **Motion that resolves, not motion that decorates.** The hero pins, the mosaic converges, headlines slide up scrubbed to scroll. Every motion finishes at a definite resting state — no ambient looping, no decorative parallax that doesn't carry meaning. The page reads like an elastic snap, not a continuous ride. *(Adopted from DESIGN.md § motion patterns; reframed for marketing surface.)*

5. **Data must read fast.** Where Acrobat's reference is creative-tool-evocative (PDF Spaces, AI Assistant, content production), semrush's reality is data-rich (keyword tables, audit scores, rank deltas, share-of-voice charts). When data appears on the marketing surface — as a stat row, a chart preview, a one-line metric — it gets the same single-clause clarity headlines get. No cluttered legends, no four-axis charts on the home page. Marketing-surface data is one number, one verb, one outcome. *(Synthesized — basis: this is the place where Acrobat's design language has to bend to semrush's product reality. Acrobat doesn't ship dashboards; semrush does. The principle keeps the register without erasing what semrush actually sells.)*

6. **Pill is the chrome.** Primary CTAs, filter chips, status badges, sign-in: all pill-shaped (75px or 999px radius). The 16px radius is for cards. The two never blur. *(Substance preserved from stardust/current/PRODUCT.md design principle 4 — semrush already used pills for CTAs; the redesign keeps that and applies the Acrobat formalism — no more 6px or 10px buttons.)*

7. **Social proof in its own room.** Customer logos stay — Booking, Samsung, Amazon, FedEx, Decathlon, Dropbox, Netflix, P&G, Shopify, TikTok. The list is long and well-known, and that's the point. The redesign moves them out from immediately under the hero (the SaaS reflex) into their own section with an eyebrow and a single-clause headline. *(Substance preserved from stardust/current/PRODUCT.md design principle 2; register applied per DESIGN.md § Don'ts.)*

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The redesign keeps semrush's existing baseline — `lang="en"` declared, "Skip to content" as the first focusable element, tokenized focus rings — and inherits the Acrobat-DS focus treatment: `outline: 2px solid #3b63fb; outline-offset: 2px` on focus-visible (no glow ring, no rounded outline-blur). The trial-blue is the focus color because it's already the signature accent — visible on white, visible on near-black, and reserved nowhere it would compete.

Token-swap-by-breakpoint replaces clamp() on display sizes (Title 1 swaps 80 → 56 → 40 across desktop / tablet / mobile rather than scaling continuously) — easier to predict for users on zoomed text and screen readers. Sentence-case headlines, no `text-transform: uppercase` on body or eyebrow copy.

Specific WCAG-level commitments are not surfaced on semrush's current home page; the redesign should re-affirm AA contrast minima explicitly. Reduced-motion: the hero mosaic-convergence and scroll-scrubbed text reveals must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — pin distance collapses to a static layout, text fades in without scrub.

## Voice & Tone Reference

The voice is **bold-direct**. Adopted from Acrobat Studio's reference register, applied to semrush's substance.

**Sentence shape.** Single-clause headlines terminating in a period. Em-dashes preferred over commas for asides. Sentence case throughout — no `text-transform: uppercase` (current semrush uses 27% uppercase headings; that's out). No exclamation marks.

**CTA labels.** Primary actions are short verbs: *Try it free*, *Start free trial*, *Buy now*, *Sign In*, *View Pricing*. Avoid current-semrush patterns like *Sign Up*, *Get insights*, *Try for free* in three-CTA stacks. The hero carries one primary, one secondary.

**Examples — current semrush register vs. target register**

| current semrush | target (Acrobat-register, semrush substance) |
|---|---|
| "Be found everywhere search happens" | "Show up wherever search happens." |
| "The leading platform to grow and measure brand visibility across virtually every digital channel" | "Measure your visibility — everywhere it counts." |
| "GET STARTED WITH SEMRUSH TODAY" (uppercase H2) | "Start measuring today." (sentence case, period) |
| "Start free trial" / "Get insights" / "Talk to sales" (3 CTAs in hero) | "Start free trial" + "Talk to sales" (paired primary/secondary; *Get insights* belongs on the input field, not the CTA stack) |
| "Find the Right Tools" (title-case H3, ambient) | "Find the right tool." (sentence case, period, single intent) |

## Photography & Imagery

The current semrush home page does not lead with photography — hero is type plus an input field, with pastel gradients and 3D illustrated UI mockups below the fold. The redesign keeps the *no-stock-photography* posture but adopts Acrobat's image surface: a multi-image mosaic in the hero (5×3 grid that converges on scroll), full-bleed photographic editorial cards below, and a 32px rounded section-bottom that lets the next layer peek through.

**Imagery shifts:** out — pastel gradient + 3D illustrated UI mockup. In — flat-photographed product screens (real Semrush dashboards), teams-at-work editorial photography, and abstract data viz where data is the subject (one chart, one metric). The brand still sells the tool, not the role of the operator — but the tool gets photographed as if it were used, not as if it were rendered.

## Content Pillars

Five pillars dominate the IA, preserved from the current site:

- **Products** — the toolset, surfaced via mega-nav (mirrors Acrobat's mega-nav-clip-morph pattern).
- **Pricing** — plan-card grid, integrated with the morphing sticky-CTA pill.
- **Resources** — blog, knowledge base, academy, news, eyeon, trending-websites. Out of scope for the marketing-site redesign; remains a content-marketing surface.
- **Free tools** — single-tool entry points for top-of-funnel SEO use cases.
- **Company** — about, stories, careers, legal.

The marketing-site redesign covers home, pricing, features, and company. The blog and resource surfaces are deliberately excluded from the redesign target — that's a different register (long-form / editorial) with different needs.
